Maria is faced with extreme poverty at home. Children from The Northern Triangle Countries
often do not have access to basic facilities and resources due to financial difficulty.

Economic Developement (GDP) in the Americas has been unfavorable to the Northern Triangle over the past 50 years.

Notice how the Northern Triangle Countries have consistently remained near the bottom.

Many children in the Northern Triangle are also plagued by Food Insecurity.
Between 25-35% of houselholds report having borrowed money to meet everyday food requirements.

Hunger is a real problem for children like Maria.

In most cases, food Insecurity is closely tied to economic vulnerability.

Food security also determines the way families spend on other aspects of their lives.

It also serves as a strong motivation for families to migrate in search of a more secure life.

Maria also constantly witnesses gang violence in her hometown on a day-to-day basis.
Central American gangs are responsible for brutal act of violence, chronic abuse of
women, forced displacement of many children and families in Maria's village.

The Journey of Life Cut Short


The poverty, hunger, and violence in her hometown causes Maria's family to undertake
the perilous journey to the US in order to seek better and safer living condition.

Maria is one of nearly 130,000 unaccompanied migrant children that entered the U.S. shelter system in 2022. This was a record.

Source: https://www.cbsnews.com/news/immigration-unaccompanied-migrant-children-record-numbers-us-shelter-system/

In many cases, unaccompanied children founded at the border started the
journey with their parents and were separated during the long dangerous trip.
